BJP, INDIA mega rallies in Mumbai; both sides step up efforts in UP

With more than half of Uttar Pradesh’s 80 Lok Sabha seats up for grabs in the last three phases, Prime Minister Narendra Modi will continue his campaign tour across the Hindi heartland state that is critical to the BJP’s prospects. On Friday, he will address three rallies in Barabanki, Fatehpur, and Hamirpur constituencies. He will then travel to Mumbai, where he will address a rally at Shivaji Park, with Maharashtra Chief Minister Eknath Shinde, Deputy CMs Devendra Fadnavis and Ajit Pawar, MNS chief Raj Thackeray, and Union Minister of State Ramdas Athawale. BJP is contesting three seats in the city, with Shinde’s Shiv Sena contesting the other three. Across the city, the INDIA bloc and Maha Vikas Aghadi (MVA) will hold a mega rally at BKC. Congress president Mallikarjun Kharge, NCP (Sharadchandra Pawar) president Sharad Pawar, Shiv Sena (UBT) president Uddhav Thackeray, Delhi CM Arvind Kejriwal, MPCC president Nana Patole and Congress Legislature Party leader Balasaheb Thorat will address the rally.
