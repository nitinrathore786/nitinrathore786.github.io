Panchayat’s ‘Vidhayak ji’ appears to take dig at Pankaj Tripathi for ‘glamourising struggle’, claims he was first choice for Sultan in Gangs of Wasseypur

Panchayat actor Pankaj Jha appeared to take a dig at his namesake, Pankaj Tripathi, when he said in a new interview that some actors enjoy glamorising their struggles. He also claimed that a disagreement with casting director Mukesh Chhabra prevented him from bagging a role in Anurag Kashyap’s Gangs of Wasseypur, and revealed after being prodded that he was up for the role of Sultan, eventually played by Pankaj Tripathi. He told Lallantop Cinema, “I don’t like the word ‘struggle’. If you’ve chosen to follow your passion, you should enjoy it, shouldn’t you? And as we’ve seen often in the industry, people like to glamourise their struggles. Some people say they sold potatoes, others say they lived in a tiny house, some say they stole another actor’s slippers. I feel every situation is a learning experience.” On The Kapil Sharma Show in 2019, Manoj Bajpayee revealed the Pankaj Tripathi once ‘stole’ one of his slippers to keep as a souvenir. Pankaj was working at a hotel where Manoj once stayed at as a guest.
